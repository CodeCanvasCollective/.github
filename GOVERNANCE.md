# CodeCanvas Collective: Governance & Contributor Ladder

At CodeCanvas Collective, we believe in meritocracy and transparency. This document outlines the different roles within our community, the responsibilities associated with each, and how contributors can "level up" to help manage the organization.

## 1. Contributor
*The lifeblood of the project. Anyone who interacts with the project in a meaningful way.*

* **Who they are:** Developers, designers, technical writers, or community members who submit code, docs, or ideas.
* **Requirements:**
    * Adhere to the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
    * Submit at least one contribution (Issue, PR, or Documentation fix).
* **Responsibilities:**
    * Report bugs and suggest features via Issues.
    * Submit Pull Requests (PRs) to fix bugs or add features.
    * Engage respectfully in community discussions.
* **Privileges:**
    * Listed in the project `README.md` contributors table (via bot).
    * Invitation to the community **Discord**.

## 2. Triager
*The first line of defense. Triagers help keep the issue tracker healthy and organized, allowing maintainers to focus on code.*

* **Who they are:** Active contributors who understand the project scope and frequently help others in issues/discussions.
* **Requirements:**
    * Active in the community for at least 1–2 months.
    * History of helpful comments on issues (reproducing bugs, clarifying requirements).
    * Nominated by an existing Maintainer or Steering Committee member.
* **Responsibilities:**
    * Label new issues (e.g., `bug`, `enhancement`, `good-first-issue`).
    * Close duplicate or invalid issues.
    * Ask clarifying questions on vague issues.
    * Review simple documentation PRs.
* **Privileges:**
    * **GitHub Triage Role:** Ability to manage issues/labels without write access to code.
    * "Triager" role badge in the community Discord.

## 3. Core Maintainer
*The stewards of the codebase. They have the authority to merge code and shape the technical direction.*

* **Who they are:** Highly trusted contributors who have demonstrated deep technical understanding and a commitment to the project's long-term health.
* **Requirements:**
    * Significant contribution history (high-quality PRs, architectural insights).
    * Demonstrated ability to review code constructively and empathetically.
    * **Sponsorship by the Steering Committee or two existing Core Maintainers.**
* **Responsibilities:**
    * **Code Review:** Review and approve/request changes on PRs.
    * **Merging:** Final authority to merge PRs into the `main` branch.
    * **Mentorship:** Guide new Contributors and Triagers.
    * Maintain the CI/CD pipeline and release process.
* **Privileges:**
    * **GitHub Write Access:** Full push/merge access to the repository.
    * Voting rights on technical roadmap decisions.
    * Access to the "Maintainers" private channel for sensitive discussions (e.g., security vulnerabilities).
    * Ability to expense project-related costs (subject to budget availability and approval).

## 4. Steering Committee (The "Admins")
*The strategic leadership. Responsible for legal, financial, and high-level project health.*

* **Who they are:** The project founders and senior-most maintainers.
* **Responsibilities:**
    * Manage the **Open Collective** finances (approving expenses).
    * Hold admin access to external accounts (Domains, SaaS keys, Social Media).
    * Resolve Code of Conduct violations and moderation escalations.
    * Set the high-level vision and yearly goals for the organization.
    * Tie-breaking vote on contentious technical decisions.
* **Privileges:**
    * **GitHub Admin Access:** Full control over repository settings and permissions.
    * Legal representation of the project.

---

### 🛌 Inactivity & Alumni Status
Life happens! If a Maintainer or Triager becomes inactive for **6 months** or longer, they may be moved to **Alumni** status. This removes their write access (for security) but keeps their name on the contributors list to honor their past work. Alumni are welcome to return to active status anytime!

---

### How to Step Up?
We love seeing contributors grow! If you are interested in becoming a **Triager** or **Maintainer**:

1.  **Be consistent:** Regular, small contributions are better than one massive PR.
2.  **Be helpful:** Help others in the issues and discussions.
3.  **Ask:** Feel free to reach out to a current maintainer and express your interest. We can define a path for you.
